Transforming Perspectives through Community Immersion

Feb 9, 2026 | Sam Writer

Marcus (Grade 11) leading a math workshop for 4th graders.

Elena (Grade 12) reflecting on the responsibility of being a role model.

Jordan (Grade 11) finding new purpose through community engagement.

For many students at West High, the classroom had become a place of friction rather than growth. Marcus, an 11th grader who once viewed school as an obstacle, found his outlook shifting not in a textbook, but in the hallways of Lincoln Elementary.

"I realized these kids were watching how I walked, how I talked. I couldn't just be 'the kid who doesn't care' anymore. I had to be the person they think I am," Marcus shared during a reflection session.

Root 2 Rise pairs at-risk high schoolers with younger students, tasking them with mentorship roles that demand consistency and accountability. For Elena, a senior who struggled with attendance, the realization that a 3rd grader was waiting for her help with reading changed everything.

By stepping into the role of the "elder," these students begin to internalize the societal rules they once resisted. They aren't just following instructions; they are upholding a standard for the next generation. As Jordan, another participant, puts it: "When you see a 10-year-old looking up to you like you're a hero, you start wanting to actually be one."
